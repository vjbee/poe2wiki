# Genesis tree

A 15-point crafting tree housed in the **Keepers of the Flame monastery**, introduced in [[patch-0.5.0]] alongside the [[breach]] rework. Lore: a Hiveborn artifact stolen by the keepers and gifted to the order by **the Dreamer**.

**Status:** 0.5.0 preview. Numbers and exact node layout may shift between preview and live release.

## Crafting model

- Tree birthens **jewelry** (and possibly other slot types — belts and currency trees are mentioned but not shown in the recap).
- Each node slot on the tree accepts a **womb gift** (dropped by Marshall of Zest and other Hiveborn encounters).
- Each womb gift requires **hive blood** to fertilise.
- Different womb gift types produce different jewelry: an amulet womb gift produces a rare amulet, a ring womb gift produces a rare ring, and so on.

## Tree progression

- Total **15 allocatable points**.
- **Free respec** — nodes can be toggled on/off at any time by clicking. No regret cost.
- Deeper allocations unlock progressively more crafting control.

## Branches (amulet example shown in source)

- **Base type control**: lock the base type of the birthed jewelry.
- **Exclusive base types**: bases unavailable anywhere else in the game — examples include an amulet that grants a free spirit skill, and an amulet with a built-in rune socket.
- **Mod control**: higher-level mod tiers; specific guaranteed mods.

## Ring crafting — new mod pools

The Genesis tree adds two entirely new mod categories to rings:

- **Caster mods** (right branch) — extra elemental damage on spells, increased unleash seal rate, and others.
- **Minion mods** (left branch) — a longstanding gap where minion builds had fewer interesting craftable mods than attack builds. The Genesis tree narrows this gap.

This is significant for character planning: builds that previously had to settle for generic ring stats now have dedicated craftable mod families.

## Other trees (mentioned, not detailed)

- **Belt tree** — e.g. a node enabling lightning damage on belts.
- **Currency tree** — e.g. nodes that allow crafting new jewel catalysts.

## Grass-wing orchid (Atlas tree side branch)

A Breach Atlas tree node causes **Zesh** to drop his head; returning the head to the Genesis tree grows a **grass-wing orchid** alongside the tree. GGG: "putting rings on its fingers seems to make it happy" — explicit hint that player experimentation will reveal what it does.
