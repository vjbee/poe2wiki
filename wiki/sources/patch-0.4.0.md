# Patch 0.4.0 — The Last of the Druids

## TL;DR
The 0.4.0 content patch (released Dec 12, 2025) launched the [[fate-of-the-vaal-league]], added the [[druid]] class with [[oracle]] and [[shaman]] ascendancies, introduced the [[talisman]] weapon class, and promoted [[abyss]] to a core mechanic. The endgame received significant rework that now serves as the baseline before 0.5's announced full endgame overhaul.

## Scope of this ingest

Per user direction, this ingest focuses on **endgame and meta-level changes** to establish a baseline for comparison against [[patch-0.5.0]] (patch notes drop May 21, 2026; release May 29, 2026). Druid content, per-skill rebalances, and per-ascendancy changes are noted but not filed in detail — see "Out of scope" below.

## Key claims — endgame

- Maps no longer scale [[pack-size]] with tier. Instead, monster [[map-effectiveness]] scales, granting non-unique monsters increased life, experience, and item drop quantity.
- [[tablet-crafting]] now caps at 4 modifiers. Crafting beyond 2 modifiers requires having defeated [[arbiter-of-ash]] in the current league.
- [[arbiter-of-ash]] no longer drops a rare corrupted Tablet. Phase transition triggers at a lower life threshold and the boss heals to full during the transition.
- Three tablet modifiers disabled: **Plundering**, **of the Pursuit**, **of Envy**.
- Two new tablet modifiers added: **Challenger's** (monster effectiveness) and **of Contest** (extra rare modifier on unique monsters).
- "Of Undertaking" re-enabled — 1 to 2 additional random modifiers on the affected map.
- Most existing tablet modifiers had their value ranges widened toward the high end (see [[meta/endgame-0.4.0]] for the diff table).
- "Of Fleeting" map modifier restricted to T11+ waystones with reduced value (10–15%, previously 21–25%).
- Bosses gained a [[boss-anti-burst]] mechanic. Damage reduction tapers from full to zero after emerge/aggro; boss movement away from spawn accelerates the falloff.
- [[abyss]] is now a core mechanic with encounters in campaign (Act 2+) and endgame, including a dedicated section on the [[atlas-passive-tree]].
- [[waystone]]s can no longer be instilled with [[liquid-emotions]].
- [[delirium]] no longer uses a reward bar. Monsters drop [[liquid-emotions]] directly; bosses guarantee a drop.
- Atlas Notables reworked: The High Road (Waystone), Xesht's Madness (Breach), three Delirium notables (Get out of my head!, Isn't it tempting?, It's not real, it's not real!). **Prayer for Wealth** removed (Greed Shrines no longer exist).
