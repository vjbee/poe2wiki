# Arbiter of Ash

Pinnacle endgame boss in Path of Exile 2. In patch 0.4.0, the Arbiter became the gating mechanism for advanced [[tablet-crafting]] — players must defeat the Arbiter in the current league to unlock tablet modifier slots 3–4.

## Role in 0.4 endgame

The Arbiter is the canonical pinnacle target. Beyond loot, the kill itself is now a prerequisite for optimized mapping, since the third and fourth tablet modifier slots are locked until first defeat.

This makes the Arbiter the actual progression bottleneck for endgame magic-find and boss-rush strategies, not a content endpoint.

## Changes in 0.4.0

- No longer drops a rare corrupted Tablet.
- Phase transition triggers at a lower life threshold than before.
- Heals to full during the phase transition.

The phase-transition heal interacts with [[boss-anti-burst]]: any pre-phase burst damage is effectively undone, and the boss re-enters the anti-burst window on emerge. Builds that rely on a single nuke phase are penalised twice.

## Interaction with boss-anti-burst

The Arbiter paces and charges during the fight. Per [[boss-anti-burst]] rules, bosses that move away from their spawn accelerate the damage-reduction falloff — so the Arbiter's mobility shortens the anti-burst window relative to a stationary boss. Sustained DPS builds still benefit; instant-kill plays do not.

## Build implications

- Burst archetypes (frostbomb stacks, single-shot bow setups) need a second-phase plan.
- Sustained DPS, channelling, totems, and minion builds remain favoured for the Arbiter fight specifically.
- Worth carrying anti-burst-aware boss tooling on any character intended for tablet farming.
